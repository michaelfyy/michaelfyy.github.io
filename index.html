Yuanyi (Michael) Feng

Undergraduate researcher in AI at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign

I am a computer science undergraduate at UIUC interested in embodied AI, physical intelligence, multimodal perception, world models, and energy-based learning. My research experience spans generative modeling, robotic eye-examination systems, reinforcement learning diagnostics, and medical imaging.

I like working on AI systems that connect strong learning algorithms with real-world sensing, action, and uncertainty. I am especially excited by models that are useful beyond benchmarks: systems that perceive robustly, adapt under distribution shift, and support reliable decision-making.

Research

Research Vision

I want to build AI systems that reason about the physical world: models that can perceive across modalities, represent uncertainty, allocate computation dynamically, and remain useful when deployment conditions differ from training conditions.

My current interests center on embodied intelligence, energy-based and flow-based generative modeling, multimodal perception, reinforcement learning diagnostics, and medical/robotic AI systems. Long term, I hope to contribute to intelligent systems that are empirically strong, scientifically grounded, and reliable in human-facing settings.
